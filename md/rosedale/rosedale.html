Richard Simmons

One of, if not the worst location in Baltimore County. Mail to apartments in Rosedale is delivered twice a week at best. Many bills are delivered after the due date, and I am still awaiting a bill that was due the 23rd of May. I have resorted to identifying the receipt date on the bill to document the ineptitude. After almost 250 years, the postal system is little improved since Franklin started it. I have personally observed one of the mail carriers stop at each building entrance, put the truck in park, and talk on the phone for 2-5 minutes before getting out to deliver the mail, all the while continuing with the phone conversation. It is not surprising that mail is so sparsely delivered when it takes ten times as long at each stop. Last Saturday, I had five pieces of mail delivered to my address; however, not to me. Interestingly enough, one of the pieces was not supposed to be delivered to my address, rather a location in West Baltimore. I printed labels and attached them indicating return to sender, not at this address; and on the one that was the wrong address, that too was indicated. The bar codes were lined out. Today, I had two of the same pieces re-delivered to my address. I waited about ten minutes to get my mail and saw the re-delivered pieces. Because the carrier is highly inefficient, still on the phone, and distracted, she was only three doors away. I walked down to her; she was still sitting in the truck continuing her phone call. I tapped on the window to get her attention. She was startled, but rolled down the window. I told her what had happened, and she retorted with a bevy of excuses, such as the recipient, had not changed his address, and they only deliver to addresses, not names. That works one time, but when the mail is corrected to say, return to sender, not at this address, Shouldn't they be returned? All of the mail was from the same place, Department of Transportation. She refused to take them. I told her I was not going to go through the same exercise again or be responsible for this other person's mail. At this point, instead of trying to rationalize with someone that couldn't care less about doing her job, I dropped the mail over the window as I would in a mail box. Shortly thereafter, I found out that the carrier had called her supervisor and claimed that I had assaulted her. I contend that the only crime committed here is fraud and theft against the United States Postal Service, and the perpetrator is obviously not me.
