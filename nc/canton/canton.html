Earl Cole-Williams
-1

I would like to share my most recent experience with the Canton post office. I recently ordered a purse from Amazon for my wife for Mother's Day. I was on the front porch when the mail carrier went by. I saw they had stopped so I walked to the mailbox to get the mail. It was storming so I almost waited for it to stop but I am glad that I did not. The mail carrier was not the normal person, who will put the package at the door or at least under the awning. No this woman had decided that she did not want to get wet by getting out of the Jeep, and could not even be bothered to let me know what she had done. When I got to the mailbox the package was laying on the edge of the muddy road in the rain. What would make anyone decide that this was a good idea? Why could she not get out of the Jeep and bring the package to the house, or blow the horn I would have went and got it. She could see me standing on the porch to the house that she is making a delivery to. If she is determined to be inconsiderate she could have even left the little note saying that I had to come to the post office and pick it up. Even giving her to the benefit of the doubt and saying that she left it on the mailbox and the wind blew it off, this was still beyond lazy. I would like to speak to someone at the post office about this but they seem to have taken their phone off the hook as I have gotten a busy signal every time I have tried to call.
1 year ago •
